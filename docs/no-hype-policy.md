# No Hype Policy

ABC4RD Academy uses blockchain education to teach verification, not
speculation.

This policy applies to README files, curriculum modules, issues, discussions,
events, outreach drafts, website copy, and partner notes.

## Rules

- No investment advice.
- No promises of profit.
- No claims that tokens are guaranteed, safe, or risk-free.
- No claims that blockchain solves a problem without explaining the trust model.
- No partnership, sponsorship, or endorsement claims without explicit public
  approval.
- No medical, clinical, safety-critical, or compliance claims without qualified
  expert review.
- No copying paid standards, reports, or proprietary content.
- No private data, credentials, or webhook URLs in public repositories.

## RWA And Tokenization Boundaries

For RWA education, always separate:

- token from legal claim;
- on-chain record from physical truth;
- attestation from independent verification;
- custody statement from ownership transfer;
- market narrative from technical architecture;
- compliance claim from legal review.

Safe wording:

```text
This module explains public tokenization concepts and risk boundaries. It is not
investment, legal, tax, or compliance advice.
```

## DAO Boundaries

For DAO education, always separate:

- governance token from legal control;
- vote from execution;
- treasury tool from fiduciary responsibility;
- community process from legal entity;
- public proposal from binding contract.

## AI And Verifiable Computation Boundaries

For AI/open compute education, always separate:

- hash from truth;
- model output from evidence;
- benchmark from real-world reliability;
- compute record from reproducibility proof;
- agent action from authorized decision.

## Applied Infrastructure Boundaries

In health, robotics, manufacturing, and nanomaterials:

- do not provide clinical instructions;
- do not provide unsafe robotics deployment instructions;
- do not claim industrial certification;
- do not publish laboratory synthesis or handling procedures;
- use synthetic or public demo data only where sensitive data may exist.

## Review Checklist

Before publishing a claim:

1. Is the source public?
2. Is the claim narrow?
3. Is the boundary explicit?
4. Is the disclaimer nearby?
5. Would a maintainer, regulator, engineer, or student read this as hype?
6. If yes, rewrite it.
