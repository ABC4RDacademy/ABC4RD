## Why The Leaders Are High

- This basket rewards toolmakers that expose deep docs, open-source repos,
  release surfaces, and usable builder-facing routes.
- OpenZeppelin stayed at the top because of its unusually durable open library
  surface even with a lower short-term momentum score than faster-moving
  infrastructure vendors.
- Alchemy, Tenderly, QuickNode, Hardhat, and Foundry all benefited from strong
  builder-facing technical materials and visible 2026 product activity.

## Official Signals Reviewed This Cycle

- Alchemy published `Solana gRPC` on May 4, 2026.
- Chainlink published `Quarterly Review: Q1 2026` on April 28, 2026 and kept a
  visible official event surface for Consensus 2026.
- Tenderly published its `MCP Server` launch note on March 23, 2026.
- OpenZeppelin remained heavily supported by its official docs, GitHub, and
  open-source transition policy.
