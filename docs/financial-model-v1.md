# ABC4RD Academy Financial Model v1

Status: draft for maintainer, legal, tax, and accounting review.

This document defines what ABC4RD Academy sells, who it sells to, how pricing
works, how language and territory packaging should be handled, and how money is
accepted. It is an operating model, not legal, tax, investment, or financial
advice.

For the full section-by-section operating picture, start with
[Financial Model Map v1](finance/financial-model-map-v1.md).

## Positioning

ABC4RD Academy sells education, review, practical labs, research briefings, and
institutional learning infrastructure.

ABC4RD Academy does not sell:

- investment products;
- token launches;
- trading signals;
- guaranteed employment;
- accredited degrees unless a separate accreditation process is approved;
- medical, legal, tax, or financial advice.

## What We Sell

| Product line | Buyer | Price anchor | Delivery |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Free public entry | Learners, contributors, reviewers | Free | README, Start Here, public docs |

## First Commercial Offers

Start with three offers only:

1. `Blockchain Foundations Cohort`
2. `Certificate Review Batch`
3. `Institutional Academy Starter Pack`

Do not launch more offers until at least one cohort and one certificate review
batch have been completed.

## Offer 1: Blockchain Foundations Cohort

Buyer:

- individual learners;
- students;
- junior developers;
- community members;
- scholarship learners sponsored by partners.

Format:

- 4 weeks;
- Course 01;
- ERC-20 localnet demo;
- weekly review;
- public learning artifact;
- optional certificate review.

Suggested pricing:

| Tier | Price | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public self-study | Free | no review, no certificate |
| Individual cohort | USD 49-149 | depends on region and support level |
| Supported cohort | USD 199-399 | includes mentor review and certificate review |
| Scholarship seat | Free or sponsored | funded by sponsor, grant, or institution |

## Offer 2: Certificate Review Batch

Buyer:

- learners who completed the required evidence;
- institutions sponsoring learners.

Format:

- monthly review window;
- evidence checklist;
- maintainer review;
- completion record if approved.

Suggested pricing:

| Tier | Price | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Test certificate | Free | early pilot only |
| Standard review | USD 25-75 | review fee, not a credential sale |
| Institution batch | USD 250-1,500 | up to a defined learner count |

Safety rule:

The fee pays for review labor and record preparation. It does not buy approval.

## Language Or Territory Structure

Use language groups first, territories second.

Reason:

- language is useful for education immediately;
- territory creates legal, tax, sanctions, payment, and consumer-protection
  questions;
- one language can serve multiple countries;
- one country can require multiple languages.

## Language Groups

| Group | Entry language | Commercial priority |
| --- | --- | --- |
| English | English | canonical source, global B2B, developer audience |
| Russian | Russian | founder network, post-Soviet education audience, review needed |
| Spanish | Spanish | Latin America and Spain, strong cohort potential |
| Arabic | Arabic | MENA education and partner potential |
| French | French | Europe, Africa, institutional use |

Every language group needs:

- native review;
- localized price display only after payment/legal review;
- preserved disclaimer;
- local partner review before paid territory-specific marketing.

## Territory Rules

Territory-specific selling requires review before launch:

- payment availability;
- tax treatment;
- consumer protection;
- sanctions and restricted-party screening;
- crypto payment legality;
- refund rules;
- language and advertising rules.

Until reviewed, sell as:

`international online education, subject to availability and compliance review`.

## Money Types

ABC4RD Academy can receive more than two kinds of value. Treat them separately.

| Type | Examples | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fiat money | USD, EUR, GBP, local currencies | primary operating revenue |
| Crypto assets | BTC, ETH, stablecoins | optional, compliance-gated payment channel |
| Grants | foundation, public-sector, research grants | scholarships, research, open-source work |
| Sponsorship | named support for bulletin/cohorts | public education support, no endorsement |
| In-kind value | cloud credits, software, venues, equipment | reduce costs, document fair value if needed |
| Service exchange | expert review, teaching, localization | use only with written scope |
| Equity/revenue share | startup studio or venture pilots | separate legal structure only |

## Fiat Payment Policy

Fiat should be the default payment method.

Recommended channels:

- bank transfer for institutions;
- card payments for individuals;
- invoice-based payments for B2B;
- scholarship sponsor invoices for sponsored learners.

Fiat records must include:

- payer;
- invoice or receipt;
- product/service;
- currency;
- amount;
- payment date;
- refund status;
- tax/accounting category.

## Crypto Payment Policy

Crypto should be optional and compliance-gated.

Allowed pilot scope:

- education payments only;
- no investment product;
- no Academy token sale;
- no custody service for learners;
- no promise of appreciation;
- no token-gated credential as proof of learning.

Preferred crypto payment categories:

| Category | Use | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stablecoin payment | international education payment | regulatory and issuer risk |
| BTC payment | aligned with Bitcoin education | volatility and accounting complexity |
| ETH payment | developer ecosystem payment | volatility and network-fee complexity |

Crypto records must include:

- payer identity where required;
- wallet address;
- transaction hash;
- asset;
- amount in asset;
- fiat value at receipt time;
- receiving wallet;
- invoice or receipt;
- refund policy;
- compliance review status.

Compliance references:

- [IRS Digital Assets](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/digital-assets)
- [IRS Digital Asset Transaction FAQs](https://www.irs.gov/individuals/international-taxpayers/frequently-asked-questions-on-digital-asset-transactions)
- [FinCEN virtual currency guidance](https://www.fincen.gov/resources/statutes-regulations/guidance/application-fincens-regulations-persons-administering)
- [ESMA MiCA page](https://www.esma.europa.eu/esmas-activities/digital-finance-and-innovation/markets-crypto-assets-regulation-mica)
- [EBA MiCA asset-referenced and e-money token page](https://eba.europa.eu/regulation-and-policy/asset-referenced-and-e-money-tokens-mica)

## Treasury Split

Default operating split:

| Bucket | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Operating fiat reserve | 60-80% |
| Contractor/teacher payments | 10-25% |
| Scholarship fund | 5-15% |
| Crypto treasury experiment | 0-10% |
| Emergency reserve | 5-10% |

Any crypto received should be converted or retained according to a documented
treasury decision. Do not hold volatile crypto needed for payroll, taxes, or
near-term operating costs.

## Pricing Rules

- Publish prices in fiat first.
- Use USD as the internal pricing reference until accounting chooses another
  base currency.
- Localize display prices only after legal/tax/payment review.
- Do not price certificates as guaranteed outcomes.
- Use scholarships openly and document who pays.
- Separate sponsor money from editorial control.
- Review prices quarterly.

## Refund Rules

Draft baseline:

| Product | Refund rule |
| --- | --- |
| Self-study | no payment |
| Cohort | refund before first live/review session |
| Certificate review | no refund after review work starts, except duplicate payment/error |
| Institution package | milestone-based refund terms in contract |
| Sponsorship | no editorial control, refund only if deliverable is not provided |

These rules require legal review before public use.

## Accounting And Compliance Notes

Digital assets and crypto payments can create tax, reporting, AML, sanctions,
consumer-protection, and accounting obligations. The Academy should treat crypto
payments as a reviewed payment method, not as a product.

Operating references for review:

- IRS digital assets guidance;
- FinCEN convertible virtual currency guidance;
- EU MiCA / ESMA and EBA crypto-asset guidance for EU-facing activity.

## First 30-Day Financial Actions

1. Approve the three first offers.
2. Pick fiat payment rails for individuals and institutions.
3. Decide whether crypto payments are `off`, `pilot`, or `enabled`.
4. Create invoice and receipt templates.
5. Create a scholarship ledger.
6. Create a crypto receipt log template if crypto pilot is approved.
7. Create one public pricing page as `draft`.
8. Review taxes, refunds, and terms before taking paid public orders.

## Decision Needed Before Public Sales

Default recommendation:

- launch free public self-study immediately;
- launch paid cohort in fiat first;
- keep crypto payments in `pilot` until legal/accounting review;
- accept grants and sponsorships only with written public-claims boundaries;
- avoid territory-specific marketing until payment and legal review is done.
