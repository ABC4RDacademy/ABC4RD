# ABC4RD Academy Financial Model and Revenue Strategy

Status: v0.1 draft.

Important: this document is an operational planning document, not legal, tax, accounting, financial, investment, or compliance advice. Before receiving significant funds, issuing paid certificates, accepting crypto donations, or selling corporate programs, ABC4RD Academy should consult qualified legal and tax professionals in the relevant jurisdiction.

## Goal

ABC4RD Academy needs a financial model that supports:

- free public learning;
- paid courses and cohorts;
- donations;
- corporate sponsorship;
- grants;
- consulting and pilot projects;
- responsible open-source infrastructure;
- transparent accounting;
- international growth.

## Core Principle

ABC4RD Academy should earn money by creating real educational value, not by promoting tokens, speculation, hype, or fake partnerships.

## Legal Entity Options

ABC4RD Academy needs one of these structures before serious revenue:

### Option A: Local legal entity

Use if the founder/team wants direct control in their jurisdiction.

Needed:

- company or nonprofit registration;
- business bank account;
- accounting;
- tax reporting;
- payment processor account;
- refund policy;
- privacy policy.

### Option B: US company route

Use only if appropriate for founder residency, banking, tax, and compliance.

Potential path:

- Stripe Atlas can help start a US company and connect business infrastructure where eligible.
- A US entity may make it easier to use Stripe, US banking partners, and global SaaS tools.

Requires:

- legal/tax review;
- owner identity verification;
- EIN;
- bank account;
- annual compliance.

### Option C: Fiscal host / Open Collective

Use if the project wants transparent donations before creating a full legal entity.

Potential path:

- create an Open Collective;
- apply to a fiscal host;
- receive donations through the host's legal/financial infrastructure;
- publish transparent budget and expenses.

Good for:

- open-source education;
- public donations;
- transparent community funding.

### Option D: GitHub Sponsors

Use for open-source support and maintainer/project sponsorship.

Requires:

- eligible account;
- sponsor profile;
- sponsorship tiers;
- bank/tax information;
- 2FA.

Good for:

- recurring supporters;
- open-source credibility;
- developer community.

## Payment Rails

| Rail | Use | Pros | Risks / limits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bank transfer | B2B, grants, large sponsorship | low cost, formal invoice | slow, bank/KYC requirements |
| Card payments via Stripe or similar | courses, certificates, memberships | fast, global cards | availability depends on country/entity |
| PayPal | donations, small payments | familiar to many users | fees, account limitations, country rules |
| Open Collective | donations, transparent budget | fiscal hosting, public ledger | host approval, fees, payout rules |
| GitHub Sponsors | open-source sponsorship | integrated with GitHub | eligibility and tax setup |
| Wise Business | international account details/transfers | multi-currency operations | availability and account rules vary |
| Bitcoin/Lightning via BTCPay Server | Bitcoin donations/payments | self-hosted, open-source, no processor fees | volatility, accounting, tax, security, compliance |
| Coinbase Commerce or similar | crypto payments via provider | easier hosted crypto flow | jurisdiction, fees, custody/compliance model |

## Crypto Policy

Start conservative:

1. Bitcoin donations only, preferably through BTCPay Server.
2. Publish a crypto donation disclaimer.
3. Do not accept anonymous large donations without review.
4. Do not accept tokens for investment promotion.
5. Do not issue an ABC4RD token for fundraising.
6. Convert part of crypto revenue to operating currency if needed for expenses.
7. Track fiat value at receipt time for accounting.
8. Keep wallet security separate from public GitHub.

Stablecoins and multi-chain payments should be deferred until legal/accounting policy is clear.

## What Not To Do

- Do not sell tokens to fund the Academy.
- Do not promise returns.
- Do not mix donations with tuition payments.
- Do not receive Academy funds into a personal account long-term.
- Do not accept large crypto donations without compliance review.
- Do not call certificates accredited degrees.
- Do not imply official partnerships without written confirmation.
