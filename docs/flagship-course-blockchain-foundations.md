# Blockchain Foundations: From Bitcoin to Verifiable Infrastructure

Status: v0.1 draft.

Time to prepare MVP: 2-5 days.

Estimated operating cost: $0-100/month for GitHub-based delivery, basic video hosting, and certificate verification pages.

## Course Positioning

`Blockchain Foundations: From Bitcoin to Verifiable Infrastructure` is the first flagship ABC4RD Academy course.

The course teaches blockchain as a discipline of verification, open-source infrastructure, cryptography, digital ownership claims, programmable coordination, and responsible deployment. It does not teach speculation, trading, or token promotion.

## Target Students

- beginner developers;
- students interested in Bitcoin, blockchain, AI, and digital infrastructure;
- researchers who need a technical map of the field;
- product builders who need to understand what blockchain can and cannot verify;
- future contributors to open-source blockchain projects.

## Learning Outcomes

After completing the course, a student should be able to:

1. explain the difference between a database, a blockchain, a token, a legal claim, and a verifiable record;
2. describe Bitcoin, Proof of Work, UTXO, signatures, blocks, mining, nodes, and self-custody;
3. explain Ethereum/EVM, smart contracts, wallets, gas, accounts, events, and testnets;
4. identify the limits of oracles, RWA tokenization, DAO governance, and on-chain records;
5. complete a small GitHub assignment and submit work through a pull request;
6. publish a small student portfolio entry;
7. understand why no production token, RWA, health, identity, or financial system should be launched without legal, security, and domain review.

## Course Structure

| Module | Title | Outcome | Practical Assignment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | What Is Trust? | Understand why societies use institutions, ledgers, signatures, audits, and verification. | Write a one-page note comparing trust in a spreadsheet, a bank ledger, and a blockchain. |
| 02 | Bitcoin and Proof of Work | Understand Bitcoin, UTXO, mining, nodes, fees, keys, and self-custody. | Draw a simple Bitcoin transaction flow and define UTXO, input, output, fee, and signature. |
| 03 | Ethereum and Smart Contracts | Understand EVM, Solidity, accounts, gas, testnets, and contract calls. | Read a simple ERC-20 contract and explain five functions in plain language. |
| 04 | Tokens, Credentials, and RWA | Understand token vs claim, NFT vs credential, legal ownership, custody, and attestations. | Evaluate one tokenization use case with the ABC4RD use-case framework. |
| 05 | DAO and Digital Governance | Understand multisig, voting, proposals, delegation, treasury, and governance risk. | Draft a simple DAO proposal with purpose, budget, risk, and decision rule. |
| 06 | Oracles, Data, and the Physical World | Understand why blockchains do not automatically know real-world truth. | Build an oracle-risk table for a fake asset registry or sensor system. |
| 07 | Privacy, Security, and Responsible Deployment | Understand keys, phishing, smart contract bugs, privacy, compliance, and audit gates. | Complete a security checklist for a testnet demo. |
| 08 | First Open-Source Contribution | Learn GitHub contribution flow and publish a student portfolio artifact. | Open a pull request with a glossary term, source note, or mini-essay. |

The file should include:

- chosen module;
- 250-500 word summary;
- 3 public sources used;
- one diagram, table, or glossary entry;
- one open question;
- disclaimer: "This is educational work and not financial, legal, medical, or investment advice."

## Certificate Criteria

To receive a draft ABC4RD Academy completion certificate, a student must:

1. complete at least 6 of 8 modules;
2. submit at least one GitHub PR;
3. publish a student portfolio entry;
4. complete one challenge;
5. pass a basic review checklist;
6. acknowledge the no-investment-advice and responsible-deployment disclaimers.
