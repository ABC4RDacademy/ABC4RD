# Blockchain Academy Curriculum

Flagship course:

```text
Blockchain Academy: From Bitcoin to Verifiable Infrastructure
```

## Course Positioning

This course teaches blockchain as a discipline of verifiable digital
infrastructure. It begins with Bitcoin and expands into smart contracts, RWA,
DAO, identity, verifiable computation, and applied infrastructure.

## Modules

| Module | Title | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | What Is Trust? | Explain claims, verification, and trust boundaries |
| 02 | Bitcoin As Public Verification | Understand UTXO, transactions, mining, nodes, custody |
| 03 | Ethereum And Programmable Settlement | Understand account model, smart contracts, EVM basics |
| 04 | Smart Contracts And Their Limits | Identify contract logic, bugs, and off-chain assumptions |
| 05 | Tokens, Stablecoins, RWA | Separate token, claim, custody, oracle, and legal boundary |
| 06 | DAO Governance | Understand voting, delegation, multisig, treasury, proposals |
| 07 | Oracles And Real-World Data | Explain the oracle problem and attestation risk |
| 08 | Privacy, ZK, Identity | Understand commitments, ZK concepts, credentials, privacy |
| 09 | AI Agents And Verifiable Compute | Connect compute records, provenance, logs, and agent actions |
| 10 | Blockchain Beyond Finance | Evaluate health, manufacturing, robotics, sensors, materials |
| 11 | Ethics, Law, Safety, IP | Apply disclaimers and review boundaries |
| 12 | First Open-Source Contribution | Make a small docs/source contribution |
